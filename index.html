Welcome to the Excel 2015 Giving Circle

A giving circle is when people pool money together, decide where to give the money, and make a positive impact through donations while also learning about community and philanthropy.

Other Excel cohorts have done this annually quite successfully, with participation rates of over 90% and collectively giving thousands of dollars to effective organizations that they care about.

We believe that Excel 2015 is a remarkable group of people with strong insight into effective charitable giving and the fortunate financial position to be able to contribute to a giving circle.

For 2021, we’re aiming for an 80%+ participation rate, with a recommended donation of $100 per person.

This site will serve as a landing page for general information and updates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the money going?

We don’t know yet! We’ll decide this together through a process where we optionally submit individual one-slide grant proposals, briefly present them in a group meeting, then vote afterwards for our top organisations. This will occur after we build a collective pool of funds, and the details will be clearly communicated shortly.

The intention for this process is for us to collectively discuss and engage with the topics and questions of where to give, rather than us fundraising for a specific cause or organization.

Our collective funds could be split amongst multiple organizations through approval of multiple grants, depending on our collective decision making.

What is the expected time commitment?

We will have an initial one hour meeting with all participants to describe next steps, then another one hour meeting to review proposals. So minimum 2hrs per year, maximum 3-4hrs per year if you would like to prepare a grant proposal or get involved further.

Why donate money?

Money is power, and can be leveraged effectively to do enormous good.

Why participate and donate specifically with Excel 2015?

Diversity of knowledge from our cohort of impressive individuals will allow us to learn and grow as individuals and donate effectively as a group.

Additionally, the Giving Circle is a great opportunity to get together and stay in touch.

What are the specific decision making mechanics?

It's not fully determined yet, but it will be well specified and covered in our first meeting. We respect your time and opinions, and are committed to presenting a fair, clear, and decisive process for proposing and deciding upon grants.

Can I match donations using my company's corporate matching policies?

Yes! If your company has corporate matching, it's very likely that FJC is already internally approved or can be internally approved as eligible for matching.

Are my donations tax-deductible?

Yes - tax receipts for donations will be provided via email to all donors.

What are the eligible organizations for our future grants?

501(c)(3) organizations are eligible (searchable here), as well as international organizations, although these may incur additional FJC fees.

What is FJC's fee structure?

FJC charges a 2% flat fee on incoming donations, as well as an additional 2% fee for PayPal and 2.25% fee for Credit Cards. No further fees are incurred for holding money in the account or granting funds. This is among the lowest fees for Collective Giving Accounts.
